Koforidua Lodge No. 42

Building Better Men in Unity, Truth & Service

Welcome to Koforidua Lodge No. 42

Koforidua Lodge No. 42, under the Grand Lodge of Ghana, was founded in 1964 by forty men of integrity inspired to “spread the spirit of goodwill” within Freemasonry and humanity. Granted a charter by the Grand Lodge of Scotland in 1963, the Lodge was consecrated in April 1964 at the Freemasons’ Hall, Accra, with Bro. Henry Reginald Annan as its first Master. In 1968, its own Freemasons’ Hall was established on A. Yirenkyi Avenue, Koforidua. Notable members have included Bros. Annan, Ofori-Atta, and Mensah. The Lodge remains dedicated to brotherhood, service, and the moral and spiritual growth of its members.

Our History

Founded in 1964 by forty men of integrity, Koforidua Lodge No. 42, under the Grand Lodge of Ghana, promotes goodwill, brotherhood, and service. Chartered by the Grand Lodge of Scotland, it built its Freemasons’ Hall in 1968 and continues to uphold Masonic values of integrity, fellowship, and community development in Koforidua.

Charity

Freemasons of Koforidua Lodge No. 42 are committed to charity and community service. Guided by the principles of brotherly love and compassion, they support education, healthcare, and social welfare initiatives, offering relief to the needy and promoting goodwill through acts of kindness that uplift and strengthen the Koforidua community.
